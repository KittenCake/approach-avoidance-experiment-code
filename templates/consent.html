We need your consent to proceed

You have been invited to take part in a research study named "Approach Avoidance Experiment"

1. Aims:

We seek to test decision making under time pressure, and how it relates to personality characteristics.

2. Selection of participants:

Healthy people of all ages can take part.

3. General information:

The project consists of a set of questions, and a computer game. The project complies with legal regulations in Switzerland. The competent ethics committee has approved the project (BASEC 2016-00068).

4. Benefit:

You will receive a monetary compensation. There is no other personal benefit from taking part in this project. Your data will help psychological science.

5. Rights:

Your participance is voluntary. You can stop participation at any time without stating any reasons. In this case, Swiss law requires us to keep the information you have already given. If you have questions about this project, please email bachlab.participants@gmail.com

6. Risks:

There is no known risk of taking part in this project.

7. Data protection:

We will ask you for personal information in this project. Once the data are transferred to the project's servers in Switzerland, only members of the project team can see all information. Representatives of regulatory bodies for ethics in research can gain access to the project information, and they are all bound to confidentiality. Any information used outside the project team will be fully anonymised and not be identifiable. We retain the right to publish fully anonymised data.

8. Source of funding:

The project is funded by the University of Zurich

bachlab.participants@gmail.com

  • I have been informed about aim, background and potential risk of the project, and I had the opportunity to contact the project team with questions.

  • I agree that regulatory bodies for ethics in research in Switzerland may gain confidential access to the study data.
